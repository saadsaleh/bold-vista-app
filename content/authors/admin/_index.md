Saad Saleh is a Research Scientist at imec, specializing in neuromorphic computing, network systems, and computer architectures. He obtained his PhD in Computer Science from the University of Groningen (Netherlands), where he researched memristor-based analog computing architectures to improve the expressiveness and energy efficiency of packet processors. Before PhD, he spent seven years conducting research in network systems, algorithms, data analysis, and in-network computing. Over the years, he has published more than 20 articles in leading IEEE and ACM venues.
